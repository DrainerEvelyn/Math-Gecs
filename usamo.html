USA Mathematical Olympiad (USAMO)

The USA Mathematical Olympiad (USAMO) is one of the most prestigious math competitions in the United States, designed to challenge the nation’s brightest mathematical minds. It is the final stage in the American Mathematics Competitions series and a gateway to representing the USA in the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO).

About USAMO

The USAMO consists of six proof-based problems over two days, testing students’ creativity, logical reasoning, and problem-solving skills. Participants are invited based on their performance in the AMC 10/12 and AIME exams.

Preparing for USAMO

  • Focus on proof-based problem-solving. Practice writing clear and rigorous solutions.
  • Study past USAMO problems and solutions. The Art of Problem Solving community has a wealth of resources and discussions.
  • Master key topics, including geometry, number theory, combinatorics, and algebra.
  • Participate in mock tests and practice sessions to build confidence and time management skills.

Tips for Success

  • Learn Proof Techniques: Focus on direct proofs, induction, contradiction, and other essential methods.
  • Break Down Problems: Decompose problems into smaller parts to make them more manageable.
  • Collaborate with Peers: Join math communities or study groups to discuss strategies and solutions.
  • Stay Consistent: Dedicate time regularly to practice and improve your skills.
